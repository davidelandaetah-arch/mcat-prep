🎯 CARS Deep Dive Strategy
01
Read for the author's argument, not facts. CARS is never about remembering details — it's about understanding why the author wrote the passage and what they're trying to prove.
02
Annotate actively with a 3-symbol system: "?" for confusing parts, "!" for strong claims, "→" for transitions/conclusions. Keep it under 5 seconds per paragraph.
03
Attack question stems, not passage first. Read the question before going back. Know exactly what you're hunting for before you scan.
04
Eliminate extreme language answers. Correct answers are often hedged ("may suggest," "tends to"). Wrong answers often say "always," "never," "proves," "disproves."
05
Practice 1 passage per day, every day — non-negotiable. Use Jack Westin free daily CARS. Don't skip even on easy days. Consistency is the only thing that moves CARS scores.
06
Pace at 8.5 minutes per passage. 9 passages × ~8.5 min = 76.5 min (leaves buffer). Flag and move — never spend more than 90 seconds on one question.

📐 Full-Length Review Protocol
Day 1 — Take the exam. Simulate real conditions. Start at 8 AM. Wear the same clothes you plan to wear on test day. Take every break. Eat the same food. This builds familiarity with the physical experience.

Day 2 — Full review (4–6 hrs minimum). Every wrong answer, every flagged answer, every answer you got right but weren't sure about. Ask: What concept was tested? What trap did I fall for? Would I get this right again tomorrow?

Day 3 — Anki creation & targeted review. Add new cards for any concept you missed. Re-read the relevant content chapter. Do 20 discrete questions in that area.
